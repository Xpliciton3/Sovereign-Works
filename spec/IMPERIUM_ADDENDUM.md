# I. Canon Governance and Naming Lock

## Canonical naming stack

Use the following hierarchy consistently in implementation. The master body of doctrine is The Imperium. The practitioner identity on the INTJ side is always the Uncrowned. The internal language is Vel'nar. The physical embodiment manual is The Warrior's Practice. The annual holy-day layer is the Uncrowned calendar. The relationship-facing interpretive guide remains the Uncrowned Guide to the Unspent.

## Decision rule

When two source files phrase the same matter differently, prefer the version that is more structurally explicit, more action-ready, and more internally consistent with the Uncrowned identity frame. Keep mythic language where it clarifies identity. Keep explanatory language where it clarifies application. Do not let either erase the other.

## Permanent consistency rule

Where cognitive functions are personified, the names remain fixed across all future materials: Varkon (Te), Sereth (Ti), Aureliane (Fe), Nemeir (Fi), Draven (Se), Thalor (Si), Caelis (Ne), and Vaelith (Ni). All ritual, calendar, app, and teaching materials must use the same names without drift.

# II. Pantheon Function-Work Doctrine

## What homage does

In this system, every rite directed toward a god changes the practitioner's relationship to the corresponding function. The change may be strengthening, integration, tempering, lawful containment, reconciliation with a shadow force, or deliberate embodiment under discipline.

## Function-work taxonomy

Strengthening feeds an underused function until it becomes more available. Integration gives a feared or exiled function a lawful place. Tempering reduces distortion in an overgrown function. Reconciliation repairs hostility between paired functions. Embodiment turns abstraction into repeated lived action.

## Operational doctrine lines

The gods are fed by practice. Whatever is fed grows. Whatever is named can be approached. Whatever is honored can be integrated. Whatever is denied rules from the dark. A demonized god is not solved by exile; it becomes useful only when named, bounded, and put to purpose.

# III. Uncrowned Rites of Passage

## The Severing

A rite of deliberate beginning used when a practitioner formally exits a prior identity, stale pattern, failed allegiance, or dead self-concept. Observation: one page naming what is being severed, one page naming what is retained, a spoken renunciation, physical disposal or sealing of the first page, and a closing line committing the retained material to future lawful use. Purpose: identity differentiation, cognitive closure, and lawful transition rather than vague self-reinvention.

## The Taking of the Name

A rite used when the practitioner first accepts the title Uncrowned as a governing identity. Observation: private reading of the preface, standing oath, first inscription of the chosen seal, and creation of a first ledger page for practice, food, language, and discipline tracking. Purpose: to move from admiration of the system into actual adoption.

## The Binding of the Hand

A rite marking serious commitment to embodied practice. Observation: cleaning and arranging the tools of the Warrior's Practice, a period of silent posture or breath work, a statement of what the body will now be trained to carry, and an inaugural logged session. Purpose: to move martial and physical practice out of fantasy and into accountable structure.

## The Vigil of the Long Night

A threshold rite for periods of collapse, grief, humiliation, betrayal, or extended uncertainty. Observation: lights reduced, no ornamental language, the Litany read in full, inventory of what remains under command, and a short next-day plan written before sleep. Purpose: preserve continuity when identity is strained.

## The Return from Ruin

A reintegration rite after a fall from discipline. Observation: three admissions only - where law failed, what remains intact, and what the next lawful act is. Then immediate resumption of one practice, one meal discipline, and one obligation. Purpose: prevent shame from turning lapse into abandonment.

# IV. Vel'nar Canonization, Audio, and Tutor Standards

## Canon pronunciation process

Because Vel'nar is document-defined rather than community-defined, pronunciation must be canonized intentionally. First create a recording list in four tiers: sound inventory, roots, high-frequency words, and lesson phrases. Second create a plain-English pronunciation sheet for each item. Third record exact approved audio. Fourth lock the approved files as canon and never let generic TTS silently overwrite them.

## Tutor requirement

A Vel'nar tutor is not adequate if it only displays text. It must let the learner hear exact canonical audio, test listening recognition, test recall, track scores, and escalate lessons based on mastery rather than mere completion.

## Testing ladder

Minimum tests: hear-and-match, hear-and-type, root recall, rule application, phrase assembly, lesson quiz, and cumulative mastery check. Suggested advancement rule: below 70 percent repeat; 70 to 84 percent pass but remain in review; 85 plus advances; 90 plus across repeated attempts marks strong mastery.

## Pronunciation-analysis roadmap

For later implementation, use approved audio as the gold standard, then add user recording, phoneme-aligned comparison, and human-readable feedback. Do not let speech analysis become the first dependency. Canon audio comes first; score comparison comes second; conversational tutoring comes third.

# V. Daily Application Architecture

## The Today spine

The app's primary experience must be a guided day, not a document library. The first screen should answer: what do I do now? Every step opens instructions, starts a timer, or logs completion. Core step types: opening, tea, breakfast, doctrine prompt, midday reset, meal, practice block, language block, evening practice, litany, close.

## Action before archive

Whenever both a reference page and an action page exist, the action page comes first. Example: Tea opens a recipe card with timer and ingredient state. The full doctrine or symbolic explanation sits behind a second tap. This rule preserves usability and prevents the app from becoming a static vault.

## Shared data objects

Core entities should include PracticeSession, MealEntry, IngredientState, GroceryItem, HolidayObservance, VelnarLesson, VelnarTestAttempt, MartialProgressEntry, DoctrineReading, and LitanyInvocation. Every entity should have created date, last edited date, notes field, and a simple status field.

# VI. Holiday-to-App Schema

## Holiday object structure

Each holy day should store: name, branch, fixed date or moveable rule, theological purpose, functional target, preparation window, required materials, observance steps, optional shortened observance, log prompts, and post-observance reflection prompts.

## Notification pattern

For fixed dates, create a three-step reminder chain: seven-day preparation prompt, one-day practical prompt, day-of observance prompt. For moveable dates, use a manual confirmation screen that asks whether the day will be kept at dawn, dusk, or both.

## Log prompts

Every holy day should capture: what was offered, what was named, what was strengthened or integrated, what resistance appeared, and what lawful action follows within twenty-four hours. This makes the calendar part of actual formation rather than sentiment.

# VII. Warrior's Practice Integration Layer

## Practice structure

The Warrior's Practice should appear in the app as a distinct module with three faces: instruction, session logging, and progression. Arts may remain separate inside the module, but they must share one logging grammar: duration, objective, composure, precision, notes, and next correction.

## Progress fields

Minimum graphable fields: total minutes per art, frequency per week, self-rated composure, self-rated precision, test or drill outcome, and streak consistency. This allows progression without pretending a mystical practice can be reduced to one number.

## Discipline review

Every seventh logged practice should trigger a brief review card asking what is improving, what is stalled, what the most common correction is, and whether the current load is too light, lawful, or excessive.

# VIII. Meal Plan and Grocery Completion Layer

## Ingredient state logic

Recipes should not only display ingredients; they should carry ingredient state. Each item can be in stock, low, or out. Low or out items should be addable to the grocery list with one tap from the recipe itself.

## Meal-practice relationship

Meals are not isolated from doctrine. Every meal page should show why the meal exists in the larger system: fuel, steadiness, anti-chaos, recovery, simplicity, or ritual support. This preserves the logic already present in the source plans.

## Weekly resupply rhythm

Add one weekly resupply review: pantry scan, low-item confirmation, and one-tap grocery generation. Purpose: remove mental overhead and make adherence easier than drift.

# IX. Missing Cross-Text Bridges

## Imperium to Vel'nar bridge

Add a short standard paragraph anywhere Vel'nar first appears: Vel'nar is not ornamental worldbuilding; it is a symbolic compression system for identity, discipline, and internal law. It exists to make concepts speakable, memorable, and ritually actionable.

## Imperium to Holy Days bridge

Add a standard transition into the calendar section: holy days are not interruptions to doctrine but scheduled concentrations of it. They exist to bring neglected themes into focused contact, preserve continuity across the year, and stop law from dissolving into mood.

## Imperium to Tending-facing guides bridge

Whenever the Uncrowned Guide to the Unspent is used, include a standing note that interpretation for relationship does not replace doctrine for self-command. It extends the system outward without diluting the inner law.

# X. Canon Planning Templates

## Quarter turn review

At the start of each quarter, record one primary law to strengthen, one shadow force to integrate, one daily practice to stabilize, one meal behavior to simplify, and one Vel'nar objective. Review again at quarter's end.

## Holy-day annual map

Maintain a single annual calendar page with holy day, preparation cue, offerings to obtain, and intended observance level: full, shortened, or witness-only. This keeps the ritual system realistic for actual life.

## Re-entry template

When discipline collapses, restart with a five-line card: what failed, what remains true, what I resume today, what I refuse to dramatize, and what next lawful act occurs before sleep.
