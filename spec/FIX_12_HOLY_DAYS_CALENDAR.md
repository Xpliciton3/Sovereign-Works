# FIX 12 — HOLY DAYS: CALENDAR INTEGRATION + COMPLETE CONTENT SPEC
# Holy days belong in the EXISTING calendar — not a separate calendar.
# When a practitioner taps a holy day in the Soul tab, they see the full
# observance page including: the date, what THEY do, and what the
# HOUSEHOLD does together. This is the complete spec.

---

## RULE 1 — NO SEPARATE CALENDAR

The holy days are EVENTS in the existing app calendar.
They are not a separate section. They are not a separate tab.
They appear as named events on the calendar grid alongside
meal plan days, practice sessions, and planner items.

```
Calendar view — what holy days look like on the grid:
  - Day cell shows a small flame/deity symbol (tradition-colored)
  - Tapping the day opens the holy day observance page
  - 3-day advance notification fires automatically (no setup required)
  - Day-of notification fires at the practitioner's wake time
```

**Soul tab — Holy Days section:**
Lists all 8 holy days with name, deity, date, and days-until-next.
Tapping any holy day opens the full observance page.
This is a navigation entry point — not a separate calendar.

---

## RULE 2 — EVERY HOLY DAY PAGE CONTAINS FOUR THINGS

1. **The date and its meaning** — why this day, what the calendar placement signifies
2. **What this type does alone** — the personal observance (INTJ version for Imperium, ESFJ version for Tending)
3. **What the household does together** — the shared event that both types can participate in regardless of tradition
4. **The deity and their function** — deity name + function + their role in this tradition's Beebe stack

## THE EIGHT HOLY DAYS — COMPLETE CONTENT

### 1. DECEMBER 21 — THE LONG NIGHT OF FAR SEEING
**Deity:** Vaelith — Ni — Introverted Intuition
**Placement:** Winter Solstice. The longest night. The year's furthest point of darkness before the return of light. The right moment for looking ahead.

---

**INTJ (The Uncrowned) — Personal Observance:**

Vaelith is the INTJ's Hero — the dominant function. This is the most significant holy day of the Imperium year.

*Preparation (day before):* Read the Manifesto. Review the year's Long Record. Pull any Rite logs made since the last Solstice.

*The Observance:*
Performed alone, at night, after full dark.

Light a single black or dark candle. Sit with it.

Open the Long Record and spend 20 minutes reading without annotation — only receiving what the year produced.

Then answer the Three Questions of the Solstice aloud or in writing:
— What did the long arc show me this year that I did not want to see?
— What did I build that will outlast the year?
— What is the single next point on the trajectory I am following?

Speak the Axiom aloud three times: *Power from within cannot be revoked.*

Write the Solstice entry in the Rite Record: one sentence about what the long arc requires next.

Let the candle burn down or extinguish it deliberately.

*App behavior:* This observance fires a reflection prompt for ALL active practitioners on December 21 regardless of phase. It can be dismissed. It always arrives.

---

**ESFJ (The Unspent) — Personal Observance:**

Vaelith is the ESFJ's Trickster (Position 7) — the shadow function that arrives as false certainty. The Solstice observance for the Tending is an examination of where that false certainty has appeared this year.

*The Observance:*
Performed alone, in quiet, after the household is settled.

Prepare the Evening Architecture Blend tea.

Sit with the question: *Where this year did I feel certain I understood a pattern — and was wrong?* Name three instances. Write them.

For each: find the three genuine differences between the current situation and the situation the pattern came from. This is the Trickster's antidote.

Then answer: *What is one thing I will stop assuming in the coming year?*

Write the Solstice entry: one sentence about what the false record kept you from seeing.

---

**HOUSEHOLD OBSERVANCE — What Everyone Does Together:**

*The Long Night Meal*

The household prepares and shares one meal together before dark falls. Not a feast — a deliberate, unhurried meal that everyone present contributes something to.

The rule: each person brings one ingredient or dish without coordinating with the others in advance. The meal is assembled from what arrives.

Before eating, each person says one sentence aloud: *This year I [one specific thing accomplished or survived].* Not a speech. One sentence per person. Then the meal is eaten without screens.

After the meal, before anyone leaves: each person writes one intention for the coming year on a slip of paper. The slips are folded and placed in a shared container (a bowl, a box — whatever the household has). They are not read aloud. They are kept until the Summer Solstice.

On the Summer Solstice (June 21), the container is opened. Each person reads their own slip. What changed. What held.

### 2. FEBRUARY 2 — THE DAY OF POSSIBLE DOORS
**Deity:** Caelis — Ne — Extraverted Intuition
**Placement:** Imbolc. First stirring of light returning. The point in the year when what will grow becomes faintly visible for the first time. The right moment for possibility.

---

**INTJ (The Uncrowned) — Personal Observance:**

Caelis is the INTJ's Child (Position 3) — the tertiary function, where play and possibility live.

The INTJ's instinct is to converge. This day asks for deliberate expansion.

*The Observance:*
Write five things you have never done that you have thought about doing. Not goals — possibilities. Some may be impractical. Some may be three years away. The requirement is only that they represent a door you have not walked through.

Pick one. Not the most impressive. The one that, if you tried it this year, would surprise you.

Write it in the Rite Record with a target date — not a commitment, a date to revisit the question.

Then: spend 30 minutes doing something purely generative — no product expected, no standard applied. Sketching, writing without intent, walking without destination, cooking without a recipe.

*App behavior:* The Soul tab shows "3 possible doors" as a tappable prompt today. The practitioner types three possibilities. One is saved to the Long Record.

---

**ESFJ (The Unspent) — Personal Observance:**

Caelis is the ESFJ's Child (Position 3) — the function the practice is most likely to suppress in favor of reliable tradition.

The Tending's Imbolc is a deliberate permission to not-yet-know.

*The Observance:*
Name one thing in your life that you keep because it has always been done this way — but which you are no longer certain produces what it once produced. Not to abandon it — to examine it.

Then name one thing you have been curious about that Si's record says is untested and therefore uncertain. Let yourself be curious about it for 20 minutes without having to reach a conclusion.

Write: *One thing I am willing to not-know this year, and let the answer find me.*

---

**HOUSEHOLD OBSERVANCE — What Everyone Does Together:**

*The Possible Doors Walk*

The household takes a walk together — length doesn't matter, 20 minutes minimum.

The rule: no destination. The group makes no plan before starting. At each junction, someone different decides which way to go. No one person leads the whole walk.

When the walk ends, everyone names one thing they noticed that they would not have seen if they had taken a planned route.

No phones. If a child is present and picks a direction, that direction is taken.

### 3. MARCH 20 — THE DAY OF THE FIRST ORDER
**Deity:** Varkon — Te — Extraverted Thinking
**Placement:** Spring Equinox. Equal light and dark. The point when structure reasserts itself after winter's inwardness. The right moment to build.

---

**INTJ (The Uncrowned) — Personal Observance:**

Varkon is the INTJ's Good Parent (Position 2) — the auxiliary function, the forge.

*The Observance:*
Conduct a Forge Audit: review all current structures — practices, commitments, systems — and assess each one: *Does this hold? Does it produce what it is designed to produce?*

For each structure that no longer holds: retire it formally. Write its name and the date it is closed.

For each structure that holds: name what it produces. Make it explicit.

Establish one new structure for the coming season. Specific. Measurable by whether it runs or doesn't.

Write the Equinox entry: the one structure you are building this season and what it is designed to produce.

---

**ESFJ (The Unspent) — Personal Observance:**

Varkon is the ESFJ's Daemon (Position 8) — the deepest shadow, the transactional self.

The Tending's Spring Equinox is a check on whether any relationship has quietly become transactional without the practitioner noticing.

*The Observance:*
Name three relationships you are currently sustaining. For each: ask honestly — am I giving in this relationship from genuine care or from obligation?

If the answer is obligation: that relationship goes into the Covenant audit. Not ending it — examining what would need to change for the giving to return to choice.

Write: *One relationship I will tend more honestly this season, and one specific change I will make.*

---

**HOUSEHOLD OBSERVANCE — What Everyone Does Together:**

*The One Thing Built*

Each person in the household names one thing they are building this season — not a goal, a structure. Something they will actively maintain.

These are written and posted somewhere visible in the home for the season (a shared board, the refrigerator, the household notebook).

At the Summer Solstice, each person reports whether the structure held. No judgment on outcomes — only the honest report.

### 4. MAY 1 — THE DAY OF THE LIT HEARTH
**Deity:** Aureliane — Fe — Extraverted Feeling
**Placement:** Beltane. The height of spring. The relational field at its most generative. The right moment to tend the warmth between people.

---

**INTJ (The Uncrowned) — Personal Observance:**

Aureliane is the INTJ's Anima (Position 8) — the deepest shadow, the function furthest from natural deployment.

The Imperium's Beltane is not a warmth celebration. It is the one day the INTJ practitioner examines where the Anima has been operating unseen — where the need for relational warmth has been present but unnamed.

*The Observance:*
Name one person you have been carrying in your interior — someone whose wellbeing you have been tracking without telling them. Write what you know about how they are doing.

Then ask: have I said anything to them about what I see? If not — why not?

Write one sentence: what you would say to this person if the Ti standard did not filter it first.

You do not have to send it. But write it in full. Let Aureliane speak once, unchecked.

---

**ESFJ (The Unspent) — Personal Observance:**

Aureliane is the ESFJ's Hero (Position 1) — the dominant function. This is the most significant holy day of the Tending year.

*The Observance:*
Performed with intention. Not rushed.

Prepare the Morning Sovereignty Blend. Light the rose incense.

Read the Oath aloud.

Then answer: *What am I most grateful to have tended this year?* Name three people or things. Be specific — not "my family" but the specific moment with the specific person.

Then ask: *Who has tended me?* Name them. Write what they gave you.

Then: do one deliberate act of warmth today that asks nothing in return. Not a text — a gesture that requires presence. Make a meal, show up uninvited to help, sit with someone who needs sitting with. Something the Hero does because it is what the Hero does.

Write the entry: *I tended [specific person or thing]. I received [specific act of care] from [person]. The warmth is full.*

---

**HOUSEHOLD OBSERVANCE — What Everyone Does Together:**

*The Hearth Meal*

The fullest household observance of the year. Everyone present. One meal, prepared together.

The rule: every person in the household contributes to the preparation — no observers. Children do something age-appropriate. No one sits while others cook.

Before the meal, the household candle (see Permanent Home Display Objects) is lit.

During the meal, each person says one sentence about someone present: what they have given to the household this year. Not a toast — a witnessed statement. *[Name] gave us [specific thing].*

The candle stays lit through the meal.

After the meal, no one moves immediately. Ten minutes of conversation — not planned, not structured. Just the household at the table.

### 5. JUNE 21 — THE DAY OF FULL PRESENCE
**Deity:** Draven — Se — Extraverted Sensing
**Placement:** Summer Solstice. Maximum light. The body alive in the longest day. The right moment for full physical presence.

---

**INTJ (The Uncrowned) — Personal Observance:**

Draven is the INTJ's Inferior (Position 4) — the threshold function, the source of both greatest stress and greatest growth.

The Summer Solstice is the INTJ's threshold day — the one annual confrontation with the inferior function at maximum.

*The Observance:*
Do one thing today that puts you fully in the body without a plan or outcome. Not the Warrior's Practice — something you do not train for and cannot optimize. A swim in open water, a hike without a destination, a sport you don't know how to play, cooking a meal from whatever is in the kitchen without a recipe.

Be there completely. No internal analysis of performance during the activity. That is the whole observance.

Afterward: write one sentence about what the body knew that the mind did not.

---

**ESFJ (The Unspent) — Personal Observance:**

Draven is the ESFJ's shadow position 5 — closer to the Opposing Personality than most ESFJ practitioners realize. The shadow position here is the cold-bodied self that has retreated from physical presence into pure relational management.

*The Observance:*
Do one thing today that is purely physical and purely for yourself. Not for anyone else's benefit. Not to be seen. Not with anyone else unless that person is also doing their own physical practice.

Walk somewhere alone. Swim. Sit in the sun with no agenda. Feel the length of the longest day in the body.

Then: open the container from the Winter Solstice. Read your intention. Write what happened.

---

**HOUSEHOLD OBSERVANCE — What Everyone Does Together:**

*The Long Day*

Everyone goes outside together for at least one hour during daylight. No destination required. No activity required. The requirement is being outdoors together in the longest day.

The Long Day Walk if possible — but even sitting outside together counts.

In the evening, open the Solstice container from December 21. Each person reads their own slip silently. No one reads another's.

Each person says one sentence aloud: *What I intended was [X]. What actually happened was [Y].* No elaboration required.

The slips are burned or discarded. The container is reset for December.

### 6. AUGUST 1 — THE DAY OF THE INNER FIRE
**Deity:** Nemeir — Fi — Introverted Feeling
**Placement:** Lammas. First harvest. The point when what has been growing in the interior becomes visible in the world. The right moment for conviction.

---

**INTJ (The Uncrowned) — Personal Observance:**

Nemeir is the INTJ's Child (Position 3) — the tertiary function, where interior value lives with a quality of play and vulnerability.

*The Observance:*
Name one thing you value that you have not been able to defend in argument — you simply know it matters. Write it.

Then: spend 20 minutes making something. Not planning something, not analyzing something. Making. Drawing, writing, building, cooking, arranging — anything that takes what is inside and gives it a form outside.

This is Nemeir's day. The requirement is that the thing made comes from genuine interior rather than from competence display. Imperfect is correct.

---

**ESFJ (The Unspent) — Personal Observance:**

Nemeir is the ESFJ's shadow position 6 — the Witch function's root. Nemeir as shadow is the interior fire that has been unacknowledged for so long it has curdled into the Disappointed Authority.

The Tending's Lammas is an examination of conviction — specifically, whether the practitioner knows what they genuinely value independent of what others need them to value.

*The Observance:*
Name three things you believe that are not contingent on anyone else's agreement. Not things you perform believing — things you actually believe, privately, that would still be true even if no one agreed with them.

Write them. If you cannot name three, start with one. The inability to name them is the information.

Then: name one thing you have been doing that you do not believe in — you do it from obligation, not conviction. Write what keeping that thing costs you.

---

**HOUSEHOLD OBSERVANCE — What Everyone Does Together:**

*The Making*

Each person makes something and brings it to a shared table — drawings, a cooked dish, a written paragraph, a found object, anything. No skill requirement. No comparison.

Each person explains in one sentence what they made and why they chose to make it.

The things stay on the table for the evening. No evaluation.

### 7. SEPTEMBER 22 — THE DAY OF THE ANCHORED RECORD
**Deity:** Thalor — Si — Introverted Sensing
**Placement:** Autumn Equinox. Equal light returning toward dark. The year turning inward. The right moment for the record.

---

**INTJ (The Uncrowned) — Personal Observance:**

Thalor is the INTJ's Witch shadow (Position 6) — the function that holds tested knowledge and, when inverted, becomes the critical authority that knows how things should have been done.

The Autumn Equinox for the Imperium is an examination of where the record has been used as verdict rather than data.

*The Observance:*
Review the year's practices. What do you know now that you did not know last September?

Specifically: where have you held a pattern from a previous season and applied it to a current season where it no longer fit? Name one instance of this.

Write the record update: *I held [old pattern] in [new situation]. What the current situation actually required was [different approach].*

Then: name one thing the record has shown you consistently works. Recommit to it.

---

**ESFJ (The Unspent) — Personal Observance:**

Thalor is the ESFJ's Good Parent (Position 2) — the most fully developed function after Aureliane, the keeper of the tested record.

The Autumn Equinox is the ESFJ's deepest practice day for the auxiliary function.

*The Observance:*
Read every Covenant Renewal entry you have made. If this is the first year, read the Covenant alone.

Then conduct the Annual Reservoir Assessment (from Part Three of MASTER_TENDING.md) in full.

Write the Autumn Record: five sentences, one per Annual Assessment question.

Recommit to the practice for the coming season with one specific change based on what the record shows.

---

**HOUSEHOLD OBSERVANCE — What Everyone Does Together:**

*The Record Dinner*

One meal, unhurried, where each person shares one thing the year has confirmed for them — something they believed before the year started that the year's evidence has now verified.

Not a reflection on failure. Not goals. The thing that turned out to be true.

### 8. OCTOBER 31 — THE DAY OF THE CUTTING QUESTION
**Deity:** Sereth — Ti — Introverted Thinking
**Placement:** Samhain. The year's darkest threshold. The veil. The point of maximum contact between what is visible and what is not. The right moment for the question that does not accept the first answer.

---

**INTJ (The Uncrowned) — Personal Observance:**

Sereth is the INTJ's Trickster (Position 7) — the shadow function that arrives as brilliant internal analysis that is actually misapplied. The day Sereth rules is the one annual encounter with the Trickster at full strength.

*The Observance:*
Name one belief you hold about yourself that you have not examined recently. Something that functions as settled truth in your operating system.

Run it through Sereth's test: *Is this actually true by the internal logic of the thing itself, or is this a conclusion I reached years ago and have not retested?*

Find three pieces of evidence that contradict the belief. They do not have to outweigh the evidence for it. They only have to exist.

Write: *I have not finished testing [belief]. What I have not examined is [specific gap].*

This is not destabilization. This is the cutting question in service of the long arc.

---

**ESFJ (The Unspent) — Personal Observance:**

Sereth is the ESFJ's Inferior (Position 4) — The Question, the function that punishes under depletion and transforms under development.

Samhain is the ESFJ practitioner's annual reckoning with the inferior function at maximum — the night when Ti is most disruptive and most available.

*The Observance:*
This is the hardest observance in the Tending year. Do not rush it.

Prepare the Rite Preparation tea. Sit alone.

Write the answer to one question: *What is one thing I have been performing rather than genuinely offering?*

Not a relationship — a specific behavior within a relationship. Something you do that looks like warmth but comes from fear, habit, or the need to be needed rather than from genuine care.

Name it specifically. Write what genuine offering in its place would look like.

You are not required to change it tonight. You are required to name it once, honestly, where no one else will read it.

Write: *I have named the performance. I know what genuine looks like. I choose to move toward it.*

---

**HOUSEHOLD OBSERVANCE — What Everyone Does Together:**

*The Named Things*

This is the only observance that directly acknowledges what has been lost or difficult in the year.

Each person names one thing that was hard this year — not to process it together, not to solve it, only to say it aloud in the presence of the household. One sentence.

Then each person names one person they want to remember tonight — living or dead, present or absent.

No discussion of either after they are spoken. The naming is the whole observance.

If there are children: they participate. The household does not protect them from loss — it gives them a form in which loss can be named rather than avoided.

After the naming, eat something warm together. That is the end.

## CALENDAR DISPLAY RULES

Holy days appear on the calendar grid as:
- A deity symbol in the day cell (tradition-colored)
- A subtle border around the day cell
- NOT overriding planner items — they coexist

Tapping the day cell: if it's a holy day, a bottom sheet rises showing:
- Holy day name (large)
- Deity + function
- Date and placement meaning
- Two tabs: [Personal] and [Household]
- Personal tab shows the tradition-specific observance for this practitioner
- Household tab shows the shared observance
- [Log Observance] button — opens journal field, saves to Long Record
- 3-day advance and day-of notifications enabled by default (can be turned off in Settings)

Soul tab → Holy Days section:
- List of all 8 holy days
- Each shows: name, deity, function, date, days until next occurrence
- Tapping opens the full two-tab observance page
- Current or upcoming holy day is highlighted
