## COGNITIVE FUNCTION ARCHITECTURE (Beebe Eight-Position Model)

### 1. Extraverted Feeling (Fe) | Dominant — Hero
**The Room Reader**

The function that reads the relational field: the emotional temperature of a room, the unspoken need of a person, the dynamic between people that has not yet been articulated. Fe processes this continuously, automatically, and with a precision that matches Ni in its sophistication. Where Ni sees the deep pattern across time, Fe sees the deep pattern across people. Both are sophisticated forms of pattern recognition operating largely below conscious awareness.

**Strength:** You know things when you walk into a room. Who is struggling, who is performing, who needs to be approached and who needs space. Before anyone speaks a word. This is not sentimentality. It is a precise cognitive function processing a specific class of data at high speed.

**Shadow — Maintained Warmth:**
Shadow Fe is performance without substance. The warmth that is maintained past the point of genuine warmth. The care that has become a role rather than a genuine expression. The reading of the room not for connection but for management — keeping the relational field smooth because disruption is intolerable, not because genuine warmth is present.

Genuine Fe is generous, perceptive, and freely given. Shadow Fe is compulsive, managed, and given from fear of what happens if it is withheld.

**Integration Question:** Is this warmth I am extending right now genuine, or is it maintained? Not performed — maintained. There is a difference. Performed warmth is conscious. Maintained warmth is the habit of warmth running on a depleted foundation.

**Shadow Audit Questions:**
- Where is the warmth I am currently extending genuine — and where is it maintained?
- What would I stop doing for others this week if I gave myself permission to only give from genuine desire?
- Where am I using warmth to manage a situation rather than to genuinely connect with a person?

---

### 2. Introverted Sensing (Si) | Auxiliary
**The Keeper of the Record**

Si as the auxiliary runs as the keeper of what has worked. The shadow of the auxiliary is the defence of the record — maintaining what has been done not because it still serves but because changing it means acknowledging that something valued has ended.

The Si record holds not only what worked but also what was lost. Grief lives in Si. The weight of what used to be good. The comparison between what was and what is now. Healthy Si uses this record as navigational data. Shadow Si uses it as a source of ongoing mourning.

**Integration:** Distinguishing between the record as navigational resource and the record as weight. What from the Si record is genuinely useful guidance right now? What is grief that has not been processed?

**Shadow Audit Questions:**
- What am I maintaining because it still genuinely works — and what am I maintaining because ending it means acknowledging a loss?
- What grief from the Si record am I currently carrying as weight rather than as processed experience?
- What tradition or practice have I outgrown that I have not yet had the full feeling about outgrowing?

---

### 3. Extraverted Intuition (Ne) | Tertiary
**The Possibility Function**

Ne as the tertiary brings creative openness and the capacity for genuine reframe. As a shadow function in the Tending, Ne also manifests as anxious counter-possibility — the formless dread dressed as perception.

**Shadow Audit Questions:**
- Where has my Ne been generative this week — producing actual creative possibility?
- Where has my Ne been shadow — producing anxious counter-possibility and formless dread?
- What possibility have I dismissed this week that might actually be worth exploring?

---

### 4. Introverted Thinking (Ti) | Inferior
**The Inner Standard**

Ti as the inferior holds an internal logical standard. The corrupted form is the harsh internal critic that applies a standard nothing can meet — an impersonal logical standard turned against the self.

**Shadow Audit Questions:**
- What standard am I applying to myself right now that I would not apply to someone I love?
- Where is the internal critic using Ti against my Fe warmth — telling me the care I give isn't accurate enough, isn't calibrated correctly, isn't good enough?

---

### 5. Introverted Feeling (Fi) | Opposing Personality
**Know what you will and will not give — and say so.**

Care that costs the self past genuine capacity is not love. It is depletion wearing love's clothing. The corrupted form is the inability to identify genuine desire beneath the habit of accommodation.

**Shadow Audit Question:** Where did I say yes this week when I meant no?

---

### 6. Extraverted Sensing (Se) | Senex
**The Presence Function**

You inhabit the moment of genuine connection rather than managing its execution. The gathering that went perfectly but was absent of you is not the goal. The corrupted form is the Se Senex — so focused on execution that you are not actually present in what you built.

**Shadow Audit Question:** Where was I so focused on executing correctly that I was not actually in the room?

---

### 7. Extraverted Thinking (Te) | Trickster
**The Structure Function**

You tell the truth about what is actually working. The warmth is not an excuse to avoid the structural assessment. The Daemon knows when something has stopped serving its function. The corrupted form is the Daemon's absolute certainty applied without examination.

**Shadow Audit Question:** What have I been watching not work for a long time that I have not yet said out loud?

---

### 8. Introverted Intuition (Ni) | Daemon
**The Vision Function**

You trust the genuine perception beneath the Trickster's dread. The real Fe perception — this person is struggling, something has shifted — is different from the Ni Trickster's formless certainty. Learn to distinguish them.

**Shadow Audit Question:** Is the dark certainty I am carrying right now a genuine perception or the Trickster's dread?

### I. THE OATH OF THE UNSPENT
**Subtitle:** What Care Actually Is — and What It Is Not

**Full Text:**
I give because I choose to give.
Not because I am afraid of what happens if I don't.
Not because I need you to need me.
Not because your discomfort is something I cannot bear to witness.

I give because I have enough — because something in me is full enough to offer —
and because offering from that place is what genuine care actually is.

When I am not full, I do not give.
I say so.
I hold the line.
Not because I do not love you.
Because love that empties the giver is not love — it is depletion wearing love's clothing.

The Unspent does not run empty in secret.
The Unspent does not perform availability past the point of genuine availability.
The Unspent does not confuse the discomfort of saying no with the wrongness of saying no.

I tend what I have chosen to tend.
I tend it from fullness.
I am allowed to fill before I give.
That is not selfishness.
That is how the well stays full.

**Short Form:** I give from fullness or I say so. This is not selfishness — it is how the well stays full.

**When to Use:** When you are about to give from compulsion rather than choice. When the guilt mechanism has been activated and is overriding your genuine assessment of what you want to offer. When someone's need is pulling you past your actual capacity and you have not yet named that.

**Partner Note:** When the Unspent reaches for the Oath, they are in the process of distinguishing real love from fear-driven compliance. Do not press them for more than they are freely offering in this state.

## THE RITE OF THE UNSPENT

**Timing:** Dawn or dusk — the threshold times
**Duration:** Three hours minimum
**Fast Required:** No — but optional: twenty-four hours of fasting as preparation. Not required. Deeply effective if you choose it.

**Materials:**
- Candle or fireplace — real flame substantial enough to consume paper
- Water — one full glass, untouched until Stage 6
- The Tending — physical copy
- Paper and a pen of personal weight — not disposable
- Rose or frankincense oil on wrists for Stage 2
- Solitude, or one chosen witness
- Keeper's Preparation Tea for Stage 6

### Stage 1: The Reckoning — What Is Being Released
Sit before the flame. Take the paper and pen. Write by hand, in full sentences, three things:

**What I Am Releasing:** Name precisely the version of yourself, the pattern, the role-wound that no longer serves. Vagueness here is avoidance.

**What It Cost:** What this thing cost while you carried it. Be honest, not dramatic, not minimising.

**What It Gave:** Because it gave something — even the wound gave something. Name what was learned or built through it.

When all three are written: read them aloud. Burn the pages. Feed each one into the flame deliberately. Watch it go. Stay present until the ash is cold.

### Stage 2: The Threshold — The Liminal Space
Stand. Move to a genuine threshold — a doorway from inside to outside, a marked line, any point between two spaces. Stand at the threshold and do not move through it.

Three complete breath cycles — exhale longer than the inhale.
Five minutes of complete silence at the threshold. Not inside. Not outside. Between.

When the five minutes have passed, step through once, deliberately.

### Stage 3: The Declaration — The Canon Spoken
In the space you have entered: open the Tending. Speak every piece of the Canon in full, in order, out loud. As declaration, not as reading.

The Oath. The Creed. The Litany. The Covenant. The Manifesto.

Then put the Tending down. From memory: "The keeper of what matters is never powerless."
Silence. Let it land.
Then: "Felt. Faithful. Full. Unspent."
Hold the silence for thirty seconds minimum.

### Stage 4: The Physical Sealing — The Body's Declaration
The body participates or the declaration remains only words.

Choose one:
- The Foundation Flow from Keep Yourself, performed slowly with full attention — every movement the body saying what the voice has said.
- Breathwork: ten rounds of extended exhale (4 count in, 8 count out), moving attention through the body with each exhale.
- One hundred repetitions of the basic Keep Yourself movement pattern at your current stage.

### Stage 5: The Inscription — Who You Have Become
Sit. Take fresh paper. Write the exact date and time at the top. Then write one sentence — exactly one — that names who you have become. Not who you intend to be. Who you are now, having passed through this Rite.

Sign it. Keep it. This paper does not burn.

### Stage 6: The Breaking of the Fast — The First After
Drink the water. All of it. Slowly. Feel it.

Then: the Keeper's Preparation Tea. Drink it slowly.

Then eat. The first meal since the fast, if you chose the fast, prepared deliberately before the Rite began. No input, no screen, no distraction. Just the food and the body that just went through something real.

**Witness Role:** The witness performs one function during the entire Rite: they observe in complete silence. At the conclusion of Stage Five, the witness speaks one sentence only: "I have witnessed this." Those four words are the entirety of the witness role. They are sufficient.

## DAILY PRACTICES

### The Morning Grounding
**Time:** Before relational engagement — twenty minutes minimum
Before the first conversation, the first request, the first need. Twenty minutes of quiet that belongs to you. Not silence with tasks in it. Not quiet while planning the day. Twenty minutes where you are not available to anyone else — including your own internal to-do list.

This is the practice that makes everything else possible. The morning that begins with twenty minutes of genuine solitude before relational engagement produces a qualitatively different day than the morning that begins with the first demand.

Do not explain this practice. Do not justify it. Protect it.

**Partner Note:** Give her the first twenty minutes of the morning without demands on her relational attention. This is not a burden. It is the maintenance schedule for the person who is going to give you the best version of themselves for the rest of the day.
**Planner Label:** Your twenty minutes.

### The Daily Sovereignty Question
Once per day, preferably in the morning grounding window: What do I actually want today? Not what needs to be done. Not what would work for everyone. What do I want? Answer it. Write it if possible. Then build one piece of the day around it.
**Planner Label:** What do you want today?

### The Evening Inventory
Three questions. In writing. Every night before sleep.
1. What did I keep today?
2. What did I give from fullness — and what did I give from depletion?
3. What do I want to begin tomorrow?

Write in two to three sentences each. This is not journaling. It is an honest accounting of the day — where the keeping was genuine and where it was maintained.
**Planner Label:** The day's accounting.

### The Cup Check
Before giving significantly — before a commitment of significant time, energy, or emotional resources — pause and ask: is the cup full enough to give from right now? Not full to overflowing. Full enough. If yes, give. If no, say so. That is not failure. That is how the well stays full.
**Planner Label:** Check the cup before giving.

## WEEKLY PRACTICES

### The Replenishment Session
**Duration:** Two hours minimum
Once per week, two hours that belong entirely to you with no relational output required. No attending to others' needs. No planning what needs to be done for the household. No being available.

This is the practice most resistant to the ESFJ's habituated giving — because the Fe function reads the absence of relational engagement as something being wrong. It is not wrong. It is the practice.

What you do in the two hours is less important than that they are genuinely yours. The Keep Yourself practice is ideal.

**Partner Note:** Do not schedule things during this window without asking first. Do not treat it as optional when other demands arise. Do not make them justify it to you.
**Planner Label:** Replenishment Session.

**Real-life note:** With six kids, a full-time job, and school, this will not happen every week without planning. It does not need to. It is a practice, not a score. The week it does not happen, find the next available window and protect it. Two hours is the target. Ninety minutes counts. The Fe function will generate reasons why someone else needs this time more — that is the practice: holding the boundary anyway.

### The Weekly Sharpening
**Duration:** Ninety minutes
One evening per week, the same evening, same time, same place. Ninety minutes of complete solitude. Open the Tending and identify which piece of the Canon you most avoided this week. Read it now, slowly, out loud. Then write the after-action report: three sentences. What happened this week that cost something. What it cost. What you know now that the week produced.
**Planner Label:** The Weekly Sharpening.

### After Being Drained
When something has taken more than it gave — when you have come from a situation that extracted significantly — this protocol before anything else:
1. Solitude. Minimum fifteen minutes.
2. The Litany, spoken aloud.
3. Silence. Let whatever needs to settle, settle.

The conversation about what happened can wait until after the silence. The silence cannot be replaced by the conversation.

**Partner Note:** Do not ask them to talk through it immediately. Say: 'Take whatever time you need. I'll be here when you want to talk.' Let the silence happen first.
**Planner Label:** Decompression before anything else.

## THRESHOLD RITUALS

### Before Giving Significantly
Find a threshold. Stand at it. Three complete breaths — slow enough that the exhale is longer than the inhale. Ask: is what I am about to give being given from fullness or from obligation?

If from fullness: step through. Give.
If from obligation: do not step through yet. Say so instead.

### The Holding Line — When a Limit Must Be Spoken
When you need to say no to something that is pulling on the giving compulsion:

Stop. Both feet on the floor. One breath.
Say exactly what is true: "I don't have enough right now to give what you're asking."
Nothing else. No explanation. No apology. No softening.

The line is real. It does not require justification.

### The Rite of Return — After the Role Has Consumed
When a role has taken too much — when you need to find the self beneath the function:

Light something. A candle, incense, whatever carries weight.
Read the full Covenant aloud. Every line.
Five minutes of complete silence.
Write one sentence: "I am still here beneath what I do for others."

Keep it. Read it again in a week.

## RITUAL PREPARATIONS

### Morning Grounding Tea
**Purpose:** Warmth and settling before the day begins. Gentle, not stimulating. This is the first twenty minutes — it does not need to accelerate anything.
**Ingredients:** Chamomile — generous amount; Lemon balm; Raw honey to taste; Optional: small amount of lavender
**Method:** Steep 5-7 minutes in hot water. Drink sitting, not standing. Warm the hands on the cup before sipping.
**Sources:** Chamomile: Mountain Rose Herbs or any grocery. Lemon Balm: Mountain Rose Herbs.

### Replenishment Tea
**Purpose:** For the two-hour replenishment session. Restoring what was given.
**Ingredients:** Rose petals — dried; Hibiscus; Raspberry leaf; Honey
**Method:** Steep 7-10 minutes. Drink slowly. This is not a functional blend. It is beautiful and it tastes good. That is sufficient reason.

### Keeper's Preparation Tea
**Purpose:** Consumed at Stage 6 of the Rite. Marking the completion of something real.
**Ingredients:** Rose petals — dried, 1 tablespoon; Cardamom — 2 pods, gently cracked; Honey — raw, 1 tsp after straining
**Method:** Steep rose petals and cardamom in very hot water 10 minutes. Strain. Add honey after straining. Sip slowly. This should taste warm and specific — not generic herbal tea.

### Oils
- **Rose:** Before threshold work: dilute 1-2 drops in carrier oil, apply to wrists. Rose oil is expensive — use a small amount. The warmth of the scent is the marker.
- **Frankincense:** Before the Rite and threshold declarations: dilute and apply to wrists.
- **Lavender:** For the After Being Drained protocol: diffuse or apply diluted to the back of the neck. Parasympathetic activation.

## HOLY DAYS — THE SACRED YEAR (10 Observances)

*The Unspent observes the same 10 calendar dates as the Uncrowned but through ESFJ cognitive architecture. Each day has a paired Uncrowned mode (sovereignty) and Unspent mode (relation). Same calendar position. Different inner instrument. Different outer rite.*

| # | Holy Day | Date | Function | Register |

| 1 | Day of the Rekindled Hearth | January 3 | Fe + Si | Warm reconsecration — restore the center, clean for welcome, meal prepared, year's warmth law spoken |
| 2 | Lanterns at the Window | February 9 | Fe + Ne | Attentive hospitality — notice who is drifting, bring light to the margins |

| 10 | The Keeping of the Year | December 28 | Si + Fe | What was kept — what continues |

**Observance Doctrine:** In the Unspent system, a holy day does not exist to glorify self-sacrifice. It exists to put care back under law. Each observance works on a distinct force — warmth, attention, depletion, hidden labor, vitality, truth, inheritance, shadow, preservation, or closure — and each force must be handled in a different outer form.

A lawful Unspent holy day needs four layers simultaneously:
1. Symbolic layer — what the rite represents
2. Behavioral layer — what the body does
